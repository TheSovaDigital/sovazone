How to submit an Instagram verification request

Instagram verification is not just about sending a form. The decision is influenced by how carefully the fields are completed, how convincing the documents look, and whether the account has relevant external mentions. The stronger and more coherent the profile looks, the stronger the request feels overall.

Where to find the request form

The exact labels can change depending on the app version, but the path usually follows the same logic:

  • Menu
  • Settings
  • Account type and tools
  • Request verification

If the labels are slightly different on your device, follow the same general route through settings and account tools.

How to fill the form more cleanly

Full name

Write the full name in English. It should match the documents you attach and the public identity that the profile represents. Avoid messy variations and keep the spelling stable.

Category

Choose the category that is closest to the real role of the account. Do not try to force a category just because it feels more impressive. A clean match is usually better than a louder mismatch.

Country

Choose the country that actually reflects where you mainly live, work, or operate. If most of your activity is tied to another country, it is often better to select that country rather than the passport country by default.

Audience

This field is usually better when it is short, natural, and written in clear English. The goal is not to sound grand, but to make the profile easy to understand.

Example:

My audience consists of people interested in social media, digital branding, and online growth.

Other names

This block can include the Russian full name, the English surname, and an English pseudonym if one is actively used. The point is to reduce ambiguity, not to overload the form.

Which links to add

After filling the main fields, you need to add supporting links. In an ideal scenario, the strongest type is media or editorial coverage that confirms public relevance outside Instagram.

These can include:

  • articles on websites;
  • interviews;
  • media publications;
  • other materials that support notability outside Instagram.

Several strong links are almost always better than a long list of random pages.

What to check before submitting

  • Does the name in the form match the documents?
  • Is the profile itself clean and consistent?
  • Does the account have an avatar, bio, and posts?
  • Do the attached links really relate to you or your brand?

The more complete and coherent the profile looks, the more convincing the request appears during review.

Conclusion

An Instagram verification request is not just a form. It is the presentation of your identity, profile structure, and public proof. Clear English naming, relevant documents, and strong external links all contribute to how the request is perceived.

If you avoid rushing and review the submission carefully before sending it, the final result looks much more credible.

Common questions

Can I apply without an international passport?

Usually yes, but if you can include it as an additional document, it often looks clearer and stronger.

Should the audience field be written in English?

In practice, that is very common, especially when the documents and the public presentation are built for an international format.

What if there are no news articles?

Add other relevant external publications and mentions that help prove the profile is known outside Instagram itself.
